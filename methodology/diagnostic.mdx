Before recommending any technology or security solution, ByteWise invests time in understanding your institution. The diagnostic phase is a structured, non-disruptive process designed to give you clarity on where you are today — and what risks you're carrying.

## What the diagnostic covers

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Technology inventory" icon="server">
    Cataloging all hardware, software, and network systems currently in use across your institution.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Security posture review" icon="shield">
    Assessing current defenses, policies, and past incidents to establish your baseline security state.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Operational workflow analysis" icon="diagram-project">
    Identifying manual processes, bottlenecks, and efficiency gaps that affect day-to-day operations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Risk identification" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Mapping operational, technological, and digital risks alongside their potential impact on your institution.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How a diagnostic session works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pre-session questionnaire">
    ByteWise sends a short questionnaire before the session to help identify relevant discussion areas and ensure your time is used efficiently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="On-site or remote session">
    A structured conversation with key staff — typically the IT coordinator, administrative leadership, and the principal or director.
  </Step>
  <Step title="System walk-through">
    A review of key systems, network access points, and current security measures to validate what was discussed and surface anything not captured in the questionnaire.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finding synthesis">
    ByteWise consolidates all findings into a structured report delivered within a defined timeframe after the session.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you receive

Following the diagnostic, your institution receives a written report covering all identified risk areas — operational, technological, and digital. The report includes a gap analysis comparing your current state against recommended practices, along with an initial set of prioritized recommendations to guide your next steps.

## Three areas ByteWise identifies in every diagnostic

<Steps>
  <Step title="Operational risks">
    Processes or conditions that could disrupt school operations — from staff workflows that depend on a single person, to systems with no backup or recovery plan.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Improvement opportunities">
    Areas where efficiency or capability can be meaningfully improved, often with tools or practices your institution already has access to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Technology gaps">
    Missing capabilities or outdated systems that create vulnerability or inefficiency — including unsupported software, unpatched devices, or absent security controls.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  The diagnostic is designed to fit your schedule. Most initial sessions run 2–3 hours and can be split across visits if needed.
</Info>
