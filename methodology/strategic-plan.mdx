The diagnostic reveals where you are. The strategic plan defines where you need to go and how to get there. ByteWise translates diagnostic findings into a prioritized, actionable roadmap that your institution can realistically execute.

## What makes a ByteWise strategic plan different

The plan is tailored to your institution's budget, staff capacity, and risk tolerance — not a generic template. Priorities are set by impact and feasibility, not by what's most technically sophisticated. The goal is a plan your team can act on, not one that sits in a drawer.

## Plan structure

<Steps>
  <Step title="Executive summary">
    Key findings and top priorities presented in non-technical language for school leadership, so decisions can be made without needing to interpret technical detail.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Risk register">
    A documented list of identified risks with severity ratings and recommended mitigations, giving your institution a clear record of what was found and what needs to be addressed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prioritized action list">
    Initiatives ranked by urgency and impact, each with effort estimates so your team can plan resources and timelines realistically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Quick wins">
    Immediate, low-effort actions that reduce risk or improve efficiency within days or weeks — building momentum from the start.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Medium and long-term roadmap">
    Phased improvements over 3–12 months with dependencies mapped, so your institution can sequence changes without creating gaps or disruption.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How priorities are set

| Priority | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| **Critical** | Immediate action required — risk of data loss or operational disruption |
| **High** | Should be addressed within 30–60 days — significant vulnerability or inefficiency |
| **Medium** | Addressable within 90 days — meaningful improvement opportunity |
| **Low** | Backlog items — nice-to-have improvements with limited near-term impact |

<Tip>
  Quick wins are a key part of the plan. Early, visible progress builds institutional confidence and demonstrates the value of the engagement.
</Tip>
